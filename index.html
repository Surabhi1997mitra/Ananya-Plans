Notes on your training
Lower back fracture
Avoid: Heavy squats, deadlifts, hip thrusts, heavy lower body work. Do: Mobility, light core work, upper body. We're protecting and healing, not testing limits.
Mobility is strength
The 3 mobility days are not "rest days" — they're active recovery and injury prevention. These accelerate your back's recovery and keep you moving daily.
Zumba is your cardio
Thursday and Saturday Zumba sessions cover your cardio needs. Don't add extra running or HIIT — you've got enough stimulus between mobility and upper body work.
Progressive overload
On upper body day, when you can do all 15 reps cleanly, add a stronger resistance band or progress to the next variation (e.g., wall push-ups → incline push-ups).
Consistency beats intensity
Show up 5 days a week. Do the work at 70% intensity. You'll get better results than sporadic 100% effort.

Red Flags & Watch-For
1 — Lower back will flare if she gets impatient
She's mentioned it's "almost recovered" but hairline fractures are fragile. If week 2 shows slow progress, she might try to add lower body work to speed things up. Pre-emptively tell her: "The mobility days ARE the workout. They're healing your back AND building strength. Don't add squats yet."
2 — Oil calories are the silent killer
She cooks at home sometimes, outside food other times. Home cooking = untracked oil. Outside food = double that. In the first check-in, physically show her how much 1 tsp oil is. Get her an oil brush. This is non-negotiable for 1,600 kcal to work.
3 — Cheat meals could become 3–4x/week
She said "twice a week" but at 22 with friends and office, social eating is high. Monitor week 1–2 check-ins. If she's going 3x+, it's either she's underestimating or feeling deprived. Adjust the plan to make it less strict, not more.
4 — Snacking is the real issue, not meals
She literally said "I eat junk at this time pls help avoid" — snacking is where she's losing the plot. Meal structure is clean (dal, paneer, rotis). But 3 PM office snack? Probably biscuits or chips. This is the #1 place to intervene.

If Weight Stalls After 2 Weeks
Don't panic. First, troubleshoot:
• Oil usage — ask her to track for 3 days. Likely culprit.
• Cheat meals — is she doing 2x or actually 3–4x?

What NOT to do: Drop calories further. That's lazy coaching. Find the leak first. Usually it's hidden oil + extra snacking.
